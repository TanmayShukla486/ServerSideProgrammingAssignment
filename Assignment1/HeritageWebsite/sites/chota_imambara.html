Chhota Imambara

Description

Do not let the name fool you. Chota Imambara is not at all small, and certainly has no less touristy appeal than any other tourist place in and near Lucknow. Also referred to as Hussainabad Imambara, it was built between 1837 and 1842, about four decades after the completion of Bara Imambara. Its gilded dome, turrets and intricate calligraphy evokes a “wow” on every sight

History

It was built per the orders of Muhammad Ali Shah, the third ruler of Lucknow, who today, along with the several members of his family, rests in tombs within the imambara itself. Mughals, during their centuries-long reign over most parts of the Indian subcontinent erected a plethora of buildings that are present even today. However, Chota Imambara has to be one of the most magnificent structures any Mughal emperor ever built in India. So, do not skip this place during your city tour in Lucknow.
